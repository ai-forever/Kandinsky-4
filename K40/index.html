Summary

Kandinsky 4.0 is a latent diffusion model capable of generating high-resolution images and videos based on text descriptions, as well as supplementing created videos with audio accompaniment. This is a continuation of the series of text-to-video generation models Kandinsky Video 1.0 and Kandinsky Video 1.1, as well as text-to-image generation models Kandinsky 3.0 and Kandinsky 3.1. Our model is able for text-to-video, image-to-video, text-to-image-to-video and video-to-audio generation. Below we give some examples of the inference regimes. Our model also has an accelerated distilled open-source version that can generate 12 second videos in 480p resolution in 11 seconds on a single GPU. The evaluation results show that Kandinsky 4.0 is one of the leading generative video models today with a free API and partially open source code. A technical report will be added soon.

Overall Pipeline

The base model pipeline include following parts:

  1. Variational autoencoder for video compression. We use the open-source implementation of 3D causal VAE from CogVideoX. We compress the video 8 times in spatial dimensions and 4 times in the time axis.
  2. Text embedder. To obtain embedding representations of textual descriptions, we use T5-V1.1-XXL.
  3. Latent diffusion denoiser has an MMDiT-like architecture. In total, our model has 21 pairs of MMDiT-blocks with local and global attention (or 42 blocks with full attention for low-resolution). The hidden state dimension is 3072.
The full model has 5B parameters and is capable of generating 12-second videos (25 latent frames) in HD resolution with a frequency of 8 FPS.
